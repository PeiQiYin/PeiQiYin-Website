"How marvelous!" my art teacher looked at my piece and said. I always paint in my spare time when I explore the world of art and my creativity. One day in my painting class, my teacher put a vase on the desk and said, "tell me your latest insights of your surroundings via the drawing of the vase."

Sitting on my chair and looking around at my classmates, I realized we share so much commonness: growing up in the same city, studying in the same school, and having the same pool of career choices for our future. The vase can represent our surroundings, which covers the central largest proportion of my painting. I rinsed the brush clean and started to outline the vase. For our commonality, the vase should be in only one color - gray, an inconspicuous but ubiquitous color. Since we are biologically different, the gray should be a gradient, from dark to light. Looking around again, I noticed that everyone had their drawing method, and their pieces were divergent from each other. It reminded me of memories of talking to my classmates who have different characteristics and would bring me various emotions, such as cheerfulness, hopefulness, and truthfulness.

To stand out their unique characteristics, I applied a three-dimensional technique: placing real daisies on top of the vase, circled by several blue butterflies. It represents the attractiveness of their characteristics. To increase the beauty of the piece, I finally sprinkled golden powder on the daisies. As the sun shines on the piece, the petal becomes luminous, and the stamen of daisies turns from yellow to gold. Brushing on the drawing board enables me to expose the beauty of humans, produce a pleasing and impressive piece by my heart, and most importantly, vividly express my understanding of the world.
